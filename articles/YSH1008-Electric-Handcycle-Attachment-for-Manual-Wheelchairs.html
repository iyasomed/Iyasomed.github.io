YSH1008 Electric Handcycle Attachment | Transform Your Manual Wheelchair

Published on: 2026-03-16 | Author: Iyasocare

Mobility is one of the most important aspects of independence for wheelchair users. While manual wheelchairs provide freedom, they often come with limitations in terms of speed, endurance, and distance. For many, navigating long distances or uneven terrain can be physically exhausting. This is where the YSH1008 Electric Handcycle Attachmentcomes in—a groundbreaking solution that transforms a standard manual wheelchair into a high-performance electric handcycle. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore everything you need to know about the YSH1008: its features, specifications, installation, safety, and why it’s becoming a must-have accessory for wheelchair users worldwide.

1. Introduction to the YSH1008

The YSH1008 is designed specifically to enhance the mobility of manual wheelchair users. By attaching to your existing wheelchair, it provides electric assistance without compromising the flexibility and convenience of your manual wheelchair.

Key Highlights:

  • Motor: 48V 800W hub motor
  • Battery: 12Ah lithium battery
  • Range: 40–50 km per charge
  • Charging Time: 4–6 hours
  • Speed Levels: Three adjustable speeds (15 km/h, 25 km/h, 30 km/h)
  • Braking System: Disc brake and hand brake
  • Wheel Size: 16 inches
  • Frame Material: Aluminum for lightweight durability

The YSH1008 offers a seamless blend of power, control, and comfort, allowing users to cover longer distances with less effort.

2. Why Choose an Electric Handcycle Attachment?

2.1 Extending Mobility and Independence

Manual wheelchair users often face physical fatigue, especially over long distances or uphill terrains. With the YSH1008, the electric motor assists in propelling the wheelchair, allowing users to travel further without tiring. This increased range encourages outdoor activities, social engagement, and independent travel.

2.2 Adjustable Speeds for Every Situation

The YSH1008 comes with a smart LCD display featuring three speed levels:

  • Level 1: 15 km/h – Ideal for casual cruising or crowded areas
  • Level 2: 25 km/h – Perfect for daily commuting
  • Level 3: 30 km/h – High-speed mode for open roads or longer distances

Users can easily adjust the speed depending on their environment, making it versatile for different scenarios.

2.3 Safety and Comfort

The device prioritizes safety with a dual braking system—a disc brake for reliable stopping power and a hand brake for precise control. The aluminum frame provides durability without adding unnecessary weight, and the waterproof LED headlight ensures visibility and safety during night rides.

3. Technical Specifications and Features

3.1 Motor and Battery

  • Motor: 48V 800W hub motor provides smooth and powerful propulsion.
  • Battery: 12Ah lithium battery ensures a range of 40–50 km. Lithium technology ensures long battery life and lightweight performance.
  • Charging: Quick and efficient 4–6 hour charging, making it convenient for daily use.

3.2 Frame and Wheels

  • Frame Material: High-strength aluminum ensures durability while keeping weight low.
  • Wheel Size: 16-inch wheels are designed for stability and smooth rides on urban roads and moderate terrain.

3.3 Compatibility

The YSH1008 is compatible with most manual wheelchairs. It supports frame tube diameters from 18mm to 33mm, making installation easy and widely accessible.

3.4 Display and Controls

  • LCD Display: Provides real-time speed and battery information.
  • Speed Levels: Three adjustable levels to match user preference and terrain.
  • User-Friendly Controls: Easy to operate, even for users with limited hand strength.

4. Installation Guide

  1. Check Compatibility: Ensure your wheelchair tube diameter is between 18mm–33mm.
  2. Attach the Frame: Secure the handcycle attachment to the front of your wheelchair frame.
  3. Connect the Motor: Attach the hub motor to the front wheel.
  4. Secure Brakes and Controls: Make sure the disc brake, hand brake, and LCD controls are properly installed.
  5. Charge the Battery: Fully charge the 12Ah battery before the first ride.
  6. Test Ride: Start with Level 1 speed in a safe area to get familiar with the controls.
